Information about blood sugar

Blood sugar testing provides useful information for managing diabetes. It can help you with the following: Monitor the effect of diabetes medications on blood sugar levels Identify high or low blood sugar levels

Most complications of diabetes result from problems in the blood vessels. Continuing high glucose levels for a long time leads to narrowing in small and large blood vessels. Narrowing limits blood flow to many parts of the body, which leads to problems. There are several reasons that lead to diabetes. Narrowing of blood vessels:

Vascular complications in diabetics Atherosclerosis leads to heart attacks and strokes. The incidence of atherosclerosis ranges between 2-4 times in younger people with diabetes, compared to healthy people of the same age group. Over time, narrowing of the blood vessels can damage the heart, brain, legs, eyes, kidneys, nerves, and skin, leading to angina, heart failure, strokes, leg cramps while walking (claudication), double vision, chronic kidney disease, and nerve damage ( Neuropathy) and skin damage.

Diabetes refers to a group of diseases that affect how the body consumes blood sugar (glucose). Glucose is an important source of energy for the cells that make up muscles and tissues. It is also the main source of energy for the brain. The main cause of diabetes varies depending on its type. But no matter what type of diabetes you have, it can lead to increased blood sugar levels. Podcast Diabetes symptoms

Mon, May 25th 2020
Diabetes symptoms depend on how high your blood sugar level is. Some people may not have symptoms, especially if they have prediabetes, gestational diabetes, or type 2 diabetes. In the case of type 1 diabetes, symptoms usually appear quickly and are more severe. Symptoms of type 1 and type 2 diabetes: Feeling thirsty more than usual and frequent urination. Unintentional weight loss. Presence of ketones in the urine. Ketones are a byproduct of the breakdown of muscle and fat that occurs when there is not enough insulin in the body. Feeling tired and weak. Easily irritable or other mood swings. Blurred vision. Slow healing of sores.
